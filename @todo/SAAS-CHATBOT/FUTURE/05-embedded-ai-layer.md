# 05 — Embedded AI layer (pluggable inside Zenvia/Blip & peers)

> Part of `SAAS-CHATBOT/FUTURE/` — strategic opportunity, **not committed scope**.
> Horizon: 🔵 Long term / exploratory. Last updated: 2026-06-14.

## Concept

Instead of only competing **against** the incumbents, be **pluggable *inside* them** — Zenvia, Blip
& peers — via their **webhook / API / marketplace / app**. Our RAG runs as the **"knowledge brain"**
**within** the support suite the customer already uses. We **sell into their installed base** (and/or
via partnership) rather than asking the customer to leave their suite.

## Why it matters (strategic)

- The incumbents own large installed bases on official channels (WhatsApp BSP). Plugging in lets us
  reach those customers **without** building channel/operational infra ourselves.
- Positions us as a **layer, not a competitor** — lower friction adoption for customers already
  invested in a suite.
- Complements (doesn't replace) our standalone product: same RAG core, new distribution.

## Possible shapes

- **Marketplace app / integration** in a player that has one (install our RAG as an add-on).
- **Webhook/API bot** registered inside their flow builder (their channel → our RAG → their reply).
- **Partnership / OEM** — they white-label our RAG brain under a revenue-share.

## Open unknowns (this is exploration, not a commitment)

- **API/marketplace openness** per player — which ones actually allow third-party AI plug-ins?
- **Revenue-share / commercial model** — viable economics vs. selling direct.
- **Platform-dependency risk** — building on someone else's platform that could change terms or
  build the feature themselves.
- **Data/privacy** — tenant content flowing through a third-party suite (LGPD, contracts).
- Prioritization: only worth pursuing **after** our standalone product + channels (`01`) are solid.

## Relationship to other vectors

- Opposite end of the spectrum from `01-channels.md` (we own the channel) — here **they** own the
  channel and we're the embedded brain. Both can coexist as distribution strategies.
